📖 How to read this — 30-second guide
What this is. Every week the big players in futures must report their positions (the CFTC "CoT" report). We track the speculators — the trend-following crowd. When they pile heavily onto one side, the move is often near exhaustion, so the edge is to lean the OTHER way — "fade the crowd" — once price confirms.
The number (0–100). Each market and complex gets a spec COT index. 0 = the crowd is the most SHORT it's been in a year; 100 = the most LONG. It's not a price — it's how stretched the crowd is.
< 20 = crowd max SHORT → lean LONG 🟢 20–80 = not crowded → stand down > 80 = crowd max LONG → lean SHORT 🔴
Why fade? At an extreme, nearly everyone who wanted in is already in — so there's little firepower left to push price further and it tends to snap back. Crowded long → vulnerable to a drop; crowded short → vulnerable to a squeeze.
Where the fade actually works (evidence-backed). From a large pooled study (every market, drift-adjusted, no lookahead): the edge is in 52-week EXTREMES held ~4 weeks, not weekly position changes (those pool to a coin-flip). And it depends on the market type — pick a market and the COT EDGE line shows its verdict: Tested per market on both lenses with Bonferroni — edges are SPARSE. The proven ones:
  • Platinum — the strongest: fade specs, follow 🔴 commercials (t=7).
  • Nasdaq — fade specs at extremes, both sides; follow commercials.
  • Silver — fade large specs when max-long. CAD — fade specs, follow commercials. Dow — fade large specs when max-long. Russell — fade retail (small specs).
  • No reliable edge (don't fade): Gold (folklore notwithstanding), Copper, Palladium, all energy, S&P 500, USD/EUR/JPY/GBP/AUD/CHF, all rates.
Amalgamated. Each card averages every market in that group (e.g. metals = gold + silver + copper + …), so one glance shows the whole complex's lean and how it shifted vs last week. The line below it is that average over time, with the 20/80 fade zones drawn in.
Greyed ≠ hidden. Markets with no proven edge still show their full positioning + chart — flagged amber when at a 52wk extreme — so you can apply your own intraday read and cross-market context (e.g. yields up → bonds down; risk-on FX vs the indices). The dashboard simply won't claim a historical edge where there isn't one — that's your call to make live.
⚠️ Setup, not a signal. A crowded reading is a heads-up, not a trade — wait for price to actually turn your way before acting. Built on a 52-week lookback; extremes at 80/20. Informational only — not financial advice.
